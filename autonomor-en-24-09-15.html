Pakhomov's Autonomor: A Philosophy of Honorable Freedom

(Sunday 15 September 2024)

Introduction

Pakhomov's Autonomor is a philosophy rooted in the principles of personal freedom, free will, and the pursuit of a life that is lived with honor and authenticity. Created by Vladimir Alexandre Pakhomov, the philosophy emphasizes the importance of autonomy and integrity in aligning one’s actions with their deepest desires and values. Autonomor encourages individuals to act with dignity, pursue their dreams, and understand the dynamic interplay between fate, free will, and human nature. In doing so, individuals can leave a meaningful impact on the world, defining their existence through their choices.

Core Principles

Autonomor is founded on four key principles: free will, truth to one’s desires, honor driven by the pursuit of dreams, and the dynamic nature of fate.

  1. Free Will as the Ultimate Guide
    Free will is the essential force that shapes our existence. In Autonomor, individuals are free to make choices that align with their personal values and ambitions. Free will is the engine of self-expression, allowing individuals to actively shape their lives and futures. By embracing one’s freedom, people have the power to pursue their dreams, confront adversity, and live authentically, despite the constraints imposed by fate.

  2. Truth to One’s Desires
    Living truthfully means aligning your actions with your deepest desires and ambitions. In Autonomor, life is not about adhering to societal expectations or surviving within predefined roles, but about self-expression and courage. Being true to yourself requires acknowledging and acting on your innermost aspirations, even if it involves challenging societal norms or confronting obstacles.

  3. Honor Driven by the Pursuit of Dreams
    Honor in Autonomor is not just about maintaining rigid ideals but is tied to the pursuit of your dreams. It’s about having the courage to act accordingly, even in complex situations where absolute truthfulness may not be possible. True honor comes from being self-aware and recognizing when your actions fall short of your ideals while still striving for alignment with your larger purpose. Honor is adaptive, allowing individuals to act in ways that serve their dreams and admit when they haven’t been entirely true to themselves.

  4. Fate as a Cyclical Force
    Fate is predetermined, but it’s not a one-way force that moves only from the past to the future. In Autonomor, fate is shaped from both the start to the end and the end to the start. This cyclical nature of fate means that the choices we make today are influenced by both the past and the future. While fate is unchangeable, it is constantly being reshaped by the choices of individuals over time. By seeking freedom, individuals participate in the creation of fate, which is predetermined but always evolving.

Human Nature and Morality

Autonomor sees human nature as a balance of self-preservation, instinct, and individual perception.

  • Instincts and Social Harmony
    While humans are driven by self-interest, their instincts and experience also contribute to social awareness and harmony. This harmony is not necessarily driven by empathy, but by aligned goals and mutual recognition of one another's autonomy. Human nature pushes individuals to avoid destructive behaviors, not out of moral obligation, but because instinct encourages survival, cooperation, and self-preservation. However, evil or harmful behaviors may always exist, as not every individual seeks harmony.

  • Subjectivity of Morality
    Morality in Autonomor is subjective and based on personal experience, not a universal standard. Human behavior is shaped by emotion and logic, but ultimately, self-interest drives actions. Honor and dignity take precedence over empathy or compassion, meaning that moral decisions are often guided by one’s own desires and vision rather than concern for others.

Social Connections and Human Emotion

  • Social Connections Beyond Empathy
    Social connections in Autonomor are not necessarily driven by empathy but by aligned goals and mutual respect. Emotions are seen as personal experiences that cannot be fully understood or connected between individuals. Human emotions are subjective, rooted in personal perceptions, and shaped by the meanings assigned to them by society. Therefore, while people may work together to achieve common goals, they do so without expecting full emotional connection or understanding.

  • Emotional Isolation and Respect for Autonomy
    In Autonomor, it is recognized that emotions are personal perceptions that cannot truly be shared or connected across individuals. People may relate to each other’s emotions based on shared experiences, but true connection is not possible because emotions are defined and experienced individually. Respect for autonomy allows for meaningful social connections, even in the absence of deep emotional bonds.

Truth, Knowledge, and Fate

Autonomor offers a dynamic view of truth, knowledge, and fate:

  • Truth as a Personal Construct
    Truth is shaped by individual perception. What one person sees as truth may not align with another’s understanding. Truth is not fixed, but fluid, shifting based on the individual’s perception of reality. People create the world they perceive, making truth both subjective and flexible.

  • Fate and the Absence of Randomness
    In Autonomor, randomness does not exist. Life is the result of choices, and fate is predetermined from both the end to the start and the start to the end. This cyclical nature of fate means that all events are intertwined, with past and future influencing one another. Fate is predetermined, but free will remains intact because individuals are unaware of their future outcomes and can act freely in the present.

  • Knowledge Expands Free Will
    Knowledge expands the number of paths available to an individual, providing them with tools to make informed choices. However, despite the expansion of will through knowledge, fate remains a product of the choices made across time. Knowledge empowers individuals, but fate still guides the larger outcome of those choices.

Society and Leadership

  • Society Built on Shared Goals
    In Autonomor, society is shaped by aligned goals. The most shared goal will determine the government or leadership of an area. Leaders are those who inspire others to follow a collective vision, and rules are created based on the most common goal of the community. However, honor remains a personal pursuit, driven by the alignment of one’s actions with their dreams and the self-awareness to remain accountable for one’s decisions.

Personal Growth and Adversity

Autonomor places great value on personal growth through self-reflection and overcoming adversity:

  • Self-Reflection as a Growth Tool
    Personal growth is achieved through constant self-reflection. This practice allows individuals to assess their actions, ensure they are on their desired path, and adjust accordingly. Realizing one’s dreams is the highest form of freedom in Autonomor.

  • Dignity in the Face of Adversity
    When adversity strikes, maintaining dignity is essential. Individuals must remain true to their values while adapting to difficult circumstances. Even when freedom seems limited, dignity allows individuals to navigate hardship with honor, finding better paths through perseverance.

Metaphysical Beliefs and Spirituality

While Pakhomov's Autonomor does not incorporate traditional metaphysical beliefs, it acknowledges that spiritual symbols can be meaningful for others:

  • Symbolism and Personal Meaning:
    Though it does not posit the existence of a metaphysical reality, the philosophy recognizes that such beliefs can serve as powerful symbols for people. Whether through envisioning a conversation with a deceased loved one or finding peace in the cessation of choices, spirituality can offer comfort and personal meaning.

Conclusion

Pakhomov's Autonomor is a philosophy that champions free will, honor, and the pursuit of a dream-driven life. It recognizes that while fate is predetermined, individuals maintain their freedom through their actions and choices. Honor comes not only from staying true to one’s ideals but from the self-awareness to recognize when flexibility is necessary. By understanding the interplay of fate, free will, and human nature, individuals can live powerful, purposeful lives that leave a lasting mark on the world.
